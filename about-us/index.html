About Us

At Check-a-Pet, we’re dedicated to enhancing the health and well-being of dogs and cats bred in the UK. Our platform is open exclusively to breeders who prioritise the welfare of their animals. We provide resources to guide responsible breeding practices, including comprehensive explanations of available health tests and their significance.

For veterinarians advising potential breeders, Check-a-Pet offers a wealth of information on best practices, health checks, and the genetics of breeding. Prospective pet owners can also find guidance here, helping them make informed choices to support conscientious breeders.

Over the years, the appearance or “design” of some breeds has been influenced by trends rather than prioritising the animals’ health and quality of life. Unfortunately, some animals suffer daily due to practices by "breeders"—those who breed solely for profit without regard for the animals' welfare.

Normal anatomy is there for a reason. Moderate shaped animals function better.

The term “extreme conformation” refers to exaggerated physical traits in pets (cats, dogs, rabbits, hoses etc) such as flat faces, bulging eyes, long backs, multiple skin folds, lop ears, extra muscles, or very small or large bodies. While these traits may be visually distinctive, they can cause significant health issues and discomfort. Moderate, well-proportioned animals generally experience fewer health problems and lead healthier lives.

Breeding responsibly starts with an evaluation of the animal’s size, shape, and overall health. After a clinical examination and health check, breeders can further assess genetic factors depending on the breed. Some diseases are unavoidable, but many can be prevented with simple blood tests or cheek swabs. These preventable health risks should be considered each time breeding is planned.

Check-a-Pet is committed to improving breeding practices in the UK and to supporting the breeding of healthy, happy pets. Join us in prioritising the future health and happiness of every pet.
